# Cloudline Stamp Press

A pocket atelier for printing small skies. Pick cloud shapes, choose a weather ink, slide dawn and breeze, then press the stamp — the page mints a named one-of-a-kind postmark from your settings.

## What it is

A self-contained, mobile-first browser toy. A postcard-shaped canvas at the top of the screen renders a painted sky. Below it sits a small atelier:

- A **cloud-shape palette** of ten silhouettes — cumulus, cirrus, stratus, anvil head, wisp, low fog, mackerel scales, tower, sunray break, and a flock of birds. Tap to toggle each one on or off; the painted sky redraws in place.
- A **weather ink** row of five colors — tidewater blue, old rose, lantern amber, twilight violet, soft graphite. The chosen ink tints the horizon, the silhouettes, and the postmark.
- A **Dawn** slider that walks the sky from "before light" through "open morning" to "noon bright," moving a sun/moon disc across an evolving gradient.
- A **Breeze** slider that pulls clouds rightward and stretches the cirrus streaks, with readouts like "soft drift," "steady breeze," and "racing sky."
- Three actions: **Shuffle Sky** (random shapes/ink/sliders), **Clear Shapes**, and the big **Press Stamp** button.

Pressing the stamp animates a small postcard card in below the controls — a named edition like *A Quiet Postmark in May* or *The First Letter Home from the Cloudline*, with the day's weather summary, the ink name, and the cloud list. There's a Copy button to save the text and a New Sky button to roll the seed and keep going.

## Privacy-safe inspiration

The mood: opening a drawer of letterpress stamps on a quiet morning and choosing which weather you want to put in the mail today. Nothing personal is referenced — just the generic fresh-start / morning-curiosity / playful-systems brief. No real names, conversations, accounts, or private details were used.

## How to open

Double-tap `index.html` in this folder. It opens in any modern mobile or desktop browser. No installation, no server, no network calls, no external assets — everything (fonts, art, randomness) is built in.

## Controls

- **Cloud Shapes (top grid)** — toggle which cloud shapes are eligible to appear in the next press. At least one shape stays on.
- **Weather Ink (row of dots)** — pick the ink color; the sky retints immediately.
- **Dawn slider** — drag from before-light to noon-bright; the gradient, sun position, and label all update live.
- **Breeze slider** — adjust the wind from "still air" to "racing sky"; clouds drift right and cirrus stretches.
- **Shuffle Sky** — re-randomizes shapes, ink, dawn, and breeze for a fresh composition.
- **Clear Shapes** — resets the palette to a single cumulus.
- **Press Stamp** — the headline action. Mints the named postmark card.
- On the result card: **Copy** copies the postmark text; **New Sky** rolls a new seed and dismisses the card.

## Why it was built

Today's Daily Surprise. The brief asked for a mobile-first generative toy distinctly different from this week's tide pool (Pocket Tide Almanac), light-routing grid (Dewdrop Switchboard), and ribbon-kite simulator (Kite Signal Atelier). A postcard press — physical metaphor, tactile sliders, immediate visual reward, and a tiny named keepsake at the end — felt like a good Sunday-morning fit.
