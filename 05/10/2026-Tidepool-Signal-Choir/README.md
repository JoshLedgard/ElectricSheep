# Tidepool Signal Choir · May 10, 2026

**What it is:** A mobile-first generative tidepool toy. Seven stones and shells ring a quiet pool. Tap any of them to give it a voice — each voice pulses light outward at its own slow tempo, and where the rings cross they brighten into an unhurried polyrhythm. The choir names itself based on which voices are awake, and a postcard captures the moment in plain text you can copy.

**Inspiration:** Crouching at the edge of a tidepool just after sunset, watching the way different stones seem to keep their own private time — one drips, one clinks, one stays silent. I wanted to bottle that into something small and thumb-friendly: a soundless choir made of light, where each pebble owns a tempo and the whole pool quietly polyrhythms itself.

**How to open:** Open `index.html` in any browser. No server, no installs, no network — works fully offline. Best on a phone, but mouse and trackpad work the same.

**Controls:**
- **Tap** a stone or shell to wake its voice. Tap again to hush that one. Stones run cool blues/teals; shells run warm pinks/golds.
- **Hush** quiets every voice at once.
- **Dawn** wakes 2–4 random voices in a gentle staggered start.
- **Postcard** opens a framed view with the choir's current name, a line of micro-prose, and a list of active voices. **Copy text** drops the whole postcard onto your clipboard.
- **Keyboard:** `1`–`7` toggle voices, `H` hushes, `D` runs Dawn, `Esc` closes the postcard.
- Reduced-motion users get the same toy with steadier, less-trail-heavy ripples.

**Why I built it:** After a kitchen toy, a timing puzzle, a text adventure, and a cartographer's lantern, I wanted something purely auditory-feeling but without sound — a pocket polyrhythm that lives entirely in light. The seven periods are deliberately co-prime-ish, so the full pattern takes a long time to repeat, and the choir's name + line are deterministic from the active voice signature: the same arrangement always names itself the same way, but the rings beneath it never quite line up the same twice.
