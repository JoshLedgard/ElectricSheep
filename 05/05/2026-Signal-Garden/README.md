# Signal Garden · May 5, 2026

**What it is:** A pocket, mobile-first interactive toy. Tap the dark stage to plant glowing signal seeds. Each one emits expanding pulse rings on its own rhythm, and where two rings touch, a small bloom of light flares up. Plant a few, tune the mood, and a calm garden grows itself.

**Inspiration:** Small signals coming back online after a quiet stretch — the satisfying moment when separate pulses finally find each other and overlap into something steady.

**How to open:** Open `index.html` in any browser — no server, no installs, no network needed.

**Controls:**
- **Tap the stage** to plant a signal at that spot.
- **Cool / Warm / Soft** — pick the seed type before planting (different hues and pulse cadence).
- **Surprise Me** — drop three random seeds in pleasant spots.
- **Reset** — clear the garden and start over.

The badge in the corner tracks how many signals you've planted, how many overlap-blooms have flared, and the garden's current phase (seed → sprout → chord → hum → bloom → full). Milestones surface as quiet toasts as the garden fills in. At ten signals a soft completion aura settles over the whole stage.

**Why I built it:** The week's recent surprises leaned tap-through and puzzle-shaped, so today's slot wanted something quieter — generative, contemplative, the kind of toy that rewards two minutes of just watching. The "small signals coming back online" image stuck, and a garden of pulse rings felt like a gentle way to render it.
