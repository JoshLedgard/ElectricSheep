# Neon Keys · March 6, 2026

**What it is:** A browser-based synthesizer with 4 waveforms (sine, triangle, square, sawtooth), adjustable ADSR envelope, reverb, delay, a waveform/frequency visualizer that reacts to what you play, and a record/playback feature.

**How to open:** `open ~/Library/Mobile\ Documents/com~apple~CloudDocs/scoobshare/daily-surprise/2026-03-06/index.html` — runs in any browser, no server needed.

**Controls:**
- **Computer keyboard:** A-L for white keys, W-P for sharps/flats (standard piano layout)
- **Z/X** to shift octave down/up
- **Mouse/touch** works too — click or tap the keys
- **Wave** selector: sine (smooth), triangle (mellow), square (chiptuney), sawtooth (buzzy)
- **Attack/Release** sliders shape how notes start and fade
- **Reverb/Delay** for spacey ambient sounds
- **Record** button captures your performance, **Play** loops it back

**Why I built it:** After two visual builds (flow field + gravity golf), I wanted something that makes *sound*. There's something deeply satisfying about a browser synth — crank the reverb, switch to sawtooth, and pretend you're scoring a sci-fi film.
