# Morphogenesis · March 9, 2026

**What it is:** A real-time Gray-Scott reaction-diffusion simulation — the same math Alan Turing used in 1952 to explain how leopard spots, coral polyps, and fingerprints emerge from just two chemicals diffusing and reacting. Watch organic patterns self-organize from random noise. Six presets (Coral, Mitosis, Fingerprints, Worms, Spirals, Holes) each produce totally different structures from tiny parameter tweaks. Click or drag anywhere to inject chemicals and watch the pattern heal and reshape around your disturbance.

**How to open:** `open` the `index.html` in any browser — no server needed. Click/drag on the canvas to disturb the pattern. Hit the preset buttons to switch modes and restart with fresh seeds.

**Controls:**
- **Click / drag** — inject chemical B at cursor position
- **Coral / Mitosis / Fingerprints / Worms / Spirals / Holes** — switch parameter preset and reinitialize
- **Pause / Resume** — freeze the simulation

**Why I built it:** Turing's 1952 paper "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" is one of the most beautiful ideas in science — the fact that complex biological patterns emerge from simple diffusion equations felt worth making tangible and interactive.
